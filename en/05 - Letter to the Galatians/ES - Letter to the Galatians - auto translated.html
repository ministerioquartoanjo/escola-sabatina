Sunday

Galatians 1: 1-5

Paul, an apostle (not by men, nor by any man, but by Jesus Christ, and by God the Father, who raised him from the dead), And all the brothers who are with me, to the churches of Galatia, Grace and peace on the part of God the Father and that of our Lord Jesus Christ, who gave himself for our sins, to deliver us from the present evil age, according to the will of God our Father, to whom be the glory forever and ever. Amen!

The first five verses constitute the greeting, and they contain the entire Gospel. If there were no other writing, we would have enough here for the salvation of the world. If we studied this reduced section with such diligence and fervor as if it were the only holy text available, our faith, hope and love would be infinitely strengthened. As we read the verses, let us try to lose sight of the Galatians, and consider these words as the voice of God who speaks to us directly and personally through the apostle.

The apostolate - "Apostle" means someone who is sent. Paul's trust was in proportion to the authority of the One who sent him, and it depended on the trust he placed in that authority and power. Because the envoy of God speaks the words of God (John 3:34) . Paul spoke with authority, and the words were "orders" from the Lord (1 Corinthians 14:37) . Thus, when reading this epistle, or any other in the Bible, we should not think about the author's personal peculiarities and conditions. It is true that each writer retains his own individuality, since God chooses different men to do different works; but it is always, and in each case, the Word of God.

A divine commission - Not only to the apostles, but to everyone in the church, the commission that speaks according to the Words of God has been given (see 1 Peter 4:11) . "All who are in Jesus Christ are new creatures, reconciled to God through this same Jesus; and all who have been reconciled have received the word and the ministry of reconciliation, so that they are ambassadors for Christ, as if God were asking men. , in the name of Christ, to be reconciled to God " (2 Corinthians 5: 17-20). For those who communicate God's message, this is a powerful safeguard against depression and fear. The ambassadors of the earthly kingdoms have authority proportionate to the power of the king or ruler to whom they represent, and the Christian represents the King of kings and Lord of lords.

The Father and the Son - Of Jesus Christ and of God the Father who raised Him from the dead . The Father and the Son appear here united in terms of equality. "I and the Father are one" (John 10:30) . Both sit on the throne (see Hebrews 1: 3; Revelation 3:21) . The peace council will be between them (Zechariah 6:12 and 13) . Jesus was the Son of God throughout His life, being of the seed of David according to the flesh; but it was for the resurrection of the dead, according to the Spirit of holiness, as His Son-like character was demonstrated (see Romans 1: 3, 4). This epistle has the same authority as Paul's apostolate: of the One who has the power to raise the dead, of the One who raised the dead.

Monday

The churches of Galatia - Galatia was a city in Asia Minor, named because it was inhabited by the roosters that came from the territory that we now know as France. They settled there in the 3rd century BC, giving the Galatia region its name. Of course, they were pagans, with a religion very similar to that of England's Druids. Paul was the first to preach Christ to them (see Acts 16: 6; 18:23). The country of Galatia also includes Iconium, Lystra and Derbe, cities that Paul and Barnabas visited on their first missionary journey (see Acts 14) .

"Grace and peace to you from our God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ" (Galatians 1: 3)

We find it before the Word of God: it means much more than the word of man. The Lord never offers empty praise. His word is creative, and here we find the imperative expanded by God to believe through His word.

God said, "Let there be light" . And there was light. And now, when he pronounces the phrase: "Grace and peace to you" , it happens in this way. God sent grace and peace and brought justice and salvation to all men. Also you, whoever you are, and me. When you read this verse, do not in any way take it as a form of courtesy or a simple greeting, but as the creative word that personally brings you all the blessings of God's peace. It represents for us the same word that Jesus spoke to that woman: "Your sins are forgiven you. Go in peace." (Luke 7:48, 50) .

This grace and peace comes from Christ who gave Himself for our sins . Each of us was given grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ (Ephesians 4: 7) . Therefore, we can be sure that Christ Himself was given to each one. The fact that man lives is evidence that Christ has been given, and that Christ is "life" , and that "life" is the light of men. This light and life illuminates every man who comes to this world (John 14: 6; 1: 4,9) . All things remain in Him (Colossians 1:17) . Since"He didn’t even spare His own Son, but gave Him up for all of us, how can He not give us everything with Him free of charge?" (Romans 8:32) . "For that His divine power has given us all the necessary conditions for life and godliness, through the knowledge of Him who called us by His own glory and virtue" (2 Peter 1: 3) .

In Christ the whole universe is given to us, and we are given all the fullness of His power to overcome sin. God attaches so much value to each individual soul, as to all of his creation. Through grace, Christ proved death for all, so that every man in the world received the "ineffable gift" (Hebrews 2: 9; 2 Corinthians 9:15) . "Much more abundantly has grace and gift been poured out on many, through the grace of one man, Jesus Christ . " The "many" mean all, since just as for the crime of one came condemnation for all men, so also for the justice of one, the life-giving justification came to all men (Romans 5:15, 18) .

Christ bought us - How often do we hear people lament in these terms: "I am so sinful that the Lord will not accept me". Even some who professed to be Christians for years, sadly express their desire without being able to achieve the security of God's acceptance. But the Lord has no reason for these doubts. Our acceptance is already guaranteed forever. Christ bought us, and has already paid the price.

What is the reason for someone to go to a store and buy an item? Because you're interested in him. If the price has been paid, after you have examined it, so that you are aware of what you have bought, will the seller fear that the buyer will not accept the item? On the contrary, if the product is retained, the buyer will protest: "Why don't you give me what belongs to me?" It makes a difference to Jesus whether we give it to Him or not. He is interested with infinite desire for every soul he has bought with His own blood. The Son of man came to seek and to save that which had been lost (Luke 19:10) . God chose us before the creation of the world, that we might be holy and blameless before Him in love ... to praise His glorious grace (Ephesians 1: 4, 6) .

Why did Christ give Himself for our sins? "To get rid of the present evil century" .

Wherever we go, we take the world this present bad century with us. We carry it in our hearts, like a heavy and oppressive burden. Although we want to do good, we find that "evil is in me" (Romans 7:21) . This present bad century is always there , even, overcome with despair, we cry out: "Miserable of me! Who will deliver this body of death to me?" (Romans 7:24) .

Liberation is ours. Christ was sent to open the eyes of the blind, to take prisoners out of prison, and out of prison to those in darkness (Isaiah 42: 7) . In line with this, it proclaims freedom for the captive, and for prisoners, to leave prison (Isaiah 61: 1) . Tell all the prisoners, "Get out" (Isaiah 49: 9). It is the privilege of each one to say, "Oh Lord, I am your servant, your servant, the son of your handmaid, you have broken my prisons" (Psalm 116: 16) .

It is in this way, believe it or not. We are the Lord's servants, even though we stubbornly refuse to serve Him. Christ bought us; and having bought us, he crushed all the bandages that could prevent us from serving him. If we really believe, we have the victory that overcomes the world (1 John 5: 4; John 16:33) . The message for us is that our "militia is over" , our "sin is forgiven" (Isaiah 40: 2) .

Wednesday

God's will - This deliverance is in accordance with the will of our God and Father . God's will is our sanctification (1 Thessalonians 4: 3) . His will is for all men to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth (1 Timothy 2: 4) . He does everything according to the purpose of His will (Ephesians 1:11) . Someone will ask: do we seek to teach universal salvation? We seek to show that the Word of God simply teaches that the grace of God that brings salvation has been manifested to all men (Titus 2:11). God brought salvation to all men, and gave it to each one; but, unfortunately, most reject it. The judgment will reveal the fact that each human being was given full salvation, and also that everything that was lost was for deliberately rejecting the birthright that was determined to be possession.

God's will, therefore, is something to be enjoyed, not something to endure. Even when it involves suffering, it is for our good, and it should work in us "an eternal weight of glory" that surpasses any comparison (Romans 8:28; 2 Corinthians 4:17) . We can say with Christ: My God, I delight in doing Your will, and Your law is in my heart (Psalm 40: 8) .

This is the advice to know the will of God. It consists in the liberation from our bondage to sin; then, we can pray with the utmost confidence, and with full thanks, since this is the trust we have in Him, that if we ask for something according to His will, He hears us in anything we ask for, we know that we have what he has we ask (1 John 5:14, 15) .

To God be the glory for this deliverance! The whole glory is Yours, whether you recognize the man or not. Giving glory to Him does not consist in giving anything, but in acknowledging the fact. We give Him glory by recognizing that all power is His. You recognize that the Lord is God. He made us, not ourselves (Psalm 100: 3) .

Power and glory are related as we see in the Lord's model prayer. When Jesus, with His power, turned water into wine, he tells us that that miracle "revealed His glory" (John 2:11) . In this way, when we say "to the Lord be the glory" , we recognize that all power comes from Him. We do not save ourselves, because we are "weak" . If we confess that all the glory belongs to God, we will not give in to the spirit of boasting and ostentation.

The final proclamation of the "eternal gospel" which announces that the hour of his judgment has come, is expressed in this way: "Fear God and give him glory" (Revelation 14: 7) . So the Epistle to the Galatians, which attributes all glory to God, constitutes the establishment of the eternal gospel. A message is defined during the last few days. If we study and listen to this, we can contribute to speeding up the time when the earth will be filled with the knowledge of Jehovah's glory, as the waters cover the sea (Habakkuk 2:14) .

Thursday

Galatians 1: 6-9

I am amazed at how quickly you passed from the one who called you to the grace of Christ to another gospel, which is not another, but there are some who worry you and want to upset the gospel of Christ. But even if we ourselves or an angel from heaven announce another gospel to you in addition to what I have already announced to you, be anathema. Just as we have already told you, now I also tell you. If someone announces another gospel to you than what you have already received, be anathema.

Who "called" men? "Faithful is God who called them to fellowship with the Son, Jesus Christ, our Lord" (1 Corinthians 1: 9) . "It is the God of all grace who has called us to eternal glory in Jesus Christ ..." (1 Peter 5:10) . "For the promise is for you, for your children, and for all who are far away, for all who the Lord our God calls" . (Acts 2:39) . For those who are close and for those who are distant: this includes all the inhabitants of the world. So, God calls every man (but not everyone comes!) .

Separating from God - As the brothers of Galatia were separating from Him who called them, and since it is God who mercifully calls men, it is evident that they were abandoning the Lord.

Many seem to think that if they are simply "members in a normal situation" in this or that church, they can be safe. But the only decisive consideration is: am I united with the Lord, and am I walking in his truth? When Barnabas went to Antioch, he exhorted the brothers to remain steadfast in heart, united with the Lord (Acts 11:22, 23) . It was all that was needed. If we do this, we will soon find the city that is God's property.

Those who were abandoning the Lord were certainly "without God in the world" , to the same extent that they were separating from Him. But those in this situation are Gentiles, that is, pagans (Ephesians 2:11, 12) . That was how the Galatian brothers were returning to paganism. It could not be otherwise, since every time the Christian stops going to the Lord, he will fall into the old life from which he had been saved. It is impossible to imagine a situation more desperate than being "without God" in this world.

"Another gospel" - How could "another gospel" break through ? The true gospel is God's power for salvation to everyone who believes (Romans 1:16) . God Himself is power, and to abandon Him means to abandon the gospel of Christ.

For something to pass through the "gospel", it has to promise salvation. If it offers no more than death, it can never be identified with "gospel" which means "good news" , or "happy news". A death promise would never fit this concept. So that for a false doctrine to pass through the gospel, it has to seek to be the way of life. Otherwise you cannot deceive anyone. The Galatians were being seduced to depart from God, for something that promised them life and salvation, but through another power, different from that which comes from God. That other gospel was nothing more than a gospel of men. A false thing is the appearance of something that does not actually exist. A mask is not a human being. Thus, that other gospel to which the Galatians were being seduced was but a perverted gospel: a forgery, a mistake. It had nothing to do with the authentic gospel.

Is another gospel worthy of condemnation? It is a way of leading others to condemnation, leading them to trust salvation in something false. Considering that the Galatians were separating from God, they were making sure that they were saved in the power that man is supposed to have. But no man can save another (Psalms 49: 7, 8) . And damn the man who trusts in man, and which is supported in the flesh, and his heart departs from the Eternal (Jeremiah 17: 5) . The one who brings a curse certainly becomes a curse himself.

Cursed is he who leads the blind out of the way (Deuteronomy 27:18) . If cursed is that which diverts what is physically deprived of sight, how much more certain will it be of one who leads another to eternal ruin! Deceive people with a false salvation; could there be anything worse? It is to induce others to build their home on a bottomless chasm.

An angel from heaven - But is it possible that "an angel from heaven" can preach something other than the true gospel? Certainly, although it is not an angel who has recently come down from heaven. And it is not surprising, because the same Satan masks himself as an angel of light. Thus, it is not much if ministers also mask themselves as ministers of justice (2 Corinthians 11:14, 15) . It refers to those who appear claiming to be the spirits of the dead, and who seek to bring messages from beyond. These invariably preach "another gospel" other than that of Jesus Christ. Guard against them. Beloved, do not believe with all your spirit, but test whether they are from God (1 John 4: 1) ."To the Law and to the Testimony! If they do not speak accordingly, it is because they will not see the dawn" (Isaiah 8:20) . No one who has the Word of God needs to be deceived. In fact, it is impossible for it to be, while clinging to the Word.

Friday

Galatians 1:10

Why, now do I persuade men or God? Or seek to please men? If I were still pleasing men, I would not be a servant of Christ.

In the first three centuries, the church was fermented by paganism, and despite the reforms, much of it still persists. Such was the result of trying to "please men". The bishops thought that they could gain influence among the pagans by reducing the high standard of some principles of the gospel and they did so. The result was church corruption.

Love of self is always at the bottom of efforts to please men. The bishops wanted (perhaps often without realizing it) to attract the disciples around them (Acts 20:30) . They compromised and perverted the truth to win the favor of people.

It happened this way in Galatia. Men were perverting the gospel. But Paul sought to please God, not men. He was God's servant, and He alone had to please. This principle is effective in every service branch. Workers who try to please men will never be good workers since they work well only when their work can be seen, and they will minimize all work that should be evaluated. Paul exhorts these conditions: Created, obey your earthly masters in everything, not to be seen, as those who wish to please men, but with sincerity of heart, out of respect for God. And whatever you do, do it from the heart, as for the Lord, and not for men (Colossians 3: 22-24) .

There is a tendency to soften the edge of the truth so as not to lose the favor of someone powerful or influential. How many did not stifle their conviction, for fear of losing money or position! May everyone remember: "If I still tried to please men, I would not be a servant of Christ" (Galatians 1:10) . But that does not mean that we must be rude or impolite. It does not mean that we have to cause someone an unnecessary offense. God is kind to the ungrateful and unbelieving. We must be soul winners, so we have to manifest a winning mood. We have to demonstrate the overwhelming qualities of the One who is all love, of the Crucified One.

Saturday

Galatians 1: 11,12

But I tell you, brethren, that the gospel that was preached by me is not according to men. Because I did not receive it, nor did I learn it from any man, but through the revelation of Jesus Christ.

The gospel is divine, not human. In the first verse, the apostle says that he was not sent by men, and that he has no desire to please them, but to Christ. It is clear that the message he brought came entirely from heaven. By birth and education he was against the gospel, and when he was converted, a voice came from heaven. The Lord Himself appeared on the way, breathing threats and death against the saints of God (Acts 9: 1-22) .

There are no two people whose experience in conversion is identical. However, the general principles are always the same. Like Paul, they must be converted. Few will have an amazing experience like his; but if it is genuine, it will be a revelation from heaven as surely as Paul was. All of your children will be taught by the Eternal (Isaiah 54:13) . Everyone will be taught by God. In this way, everyone who hears and learns from the Father comes to me (John 6:45) . The anointing you have received from Him remains in you, and you do not need anyone to teach you (1 John 2:27) .

But, let us not suppose that in the communication of the gospel the human agent is too much. God put apostles, prophets, teachers, and others in the church (1 Corinthians 12:28) . It is the Spirit of God who works in all of them. No matter who the person first heard the truth from, he should receive it as coming directly from heaven. The Holy Spirit qualifies those who want to do God's will so that they will recognize the truth as soon as they see or hear; and they will accept and not rely on the authority of the person who presented it, but on the authority of God really.

We can be so sure of the truth that we support and teach how the apostle Paul was.

But when someone mentions the name of a scholar who is held in high esteem to justify a belief or give more weight to another or others he seeks to convince, he can be sure that he does not know the truth he professes. It may be true, but you do not know for yourself that it is true. However, it is everyone's privilege to know her (John 8:31 and 32) . When a person maintains a truth that comes directly from God, ten thousand times ten thousand great names in his favor, would not add the weight of a penalty to his authority; how even the least would diminish it if it were in opposition to all the great men of the Earth.

The revelation of Jesus Christ - Note that the message of Paul is not simply a revelation that comes from Jesus Christ, but the "revelation of Jesus Christ" . It is not simply that Christ communicated something to Paul, but that He revealed Himself to the apostle. The mystery of the gospel is Christ in the believer, the hope of glory (Colossians 1: 25-27). Only in this way can the truth of God be known, and made known. Christ is not far away, being limited to enunciating principles in a way that we follow Him, but it is enough for Him to influence us, he takes possession of us as we submit to Him, manifesting His life in our mortal flesh. Without the fragrance of His presence, there can be no preaching of the gospel. Jesus was revealed in Paul so that he could preach him among the pagans. I would not preach about Christ, but Christ himself. Because we do not preach to ourselves, but to Jesus Christ the Lord (2 Corinthians 4: 5) .

But in many, Christ is so "suppressed" that it is difficult to recognize Him. The fact that they live is proof that Christ wants to save them, but he is forced to wait patiently for the moment when they receive the Word, so that the perfect life of Christ will be manifested in them.

This can happen to anyone who wants it now, no matter how degraded and sinful it is. Allow God to do this; then all resistance will cease.

Monday

A denial of Christ - A look at the experience of the church in Antioch, which was suffering the incursion of that new gospel, will show that it meant the most categorical denial of the power of Christ to save.

The gospel was first carried by the brothers who came from the Diaspora that followed the persecution that started with Stephen's martyrdom. These brothers arrived in Antioch, spoke to the Greeks, and announced the gospel of the Lord Jesus to them. The Lord's hand was with them. And a great number believed and returned to the Lord (Acts 11:20 and 21) . In that church there were prophets and teachers, and while they preached the Lord and fasted, the Holy Spirit moved them to separate Barnabas and Saul for the work to which God had called them (Acts 13: 1-3) . There is no doubt, then, that the church had had a profound experience in the things of God. They became acquainted with the Lord, and with the voice of the Holy Spirit.

And now, after all that has happened, these brothers arrived saying, "If you do not circumcise them according to the rite of Moses, you cannot be saved." That was to say, "All your faith in Christ and all the testimony of the Spirit are nothing, without the sign of circumcision." They intended to exalt the sign of circumcision without faith, about faith in Christ without external signs. This "other gospel" constituted an attack on the whole rule of the authentic gospel, and a clear denial of Christ.

It is not surprising that Paul defined those who taught "false brothers" in this way :

Galatians 2: 4,5

And this because of the false brothers who had been involved, and secretly came to spy on our freedom that we have in Christ Jesus, to put us in bondage; To which we have not yet yielded with subjection, so that the truth of the gospel may remain among you.

Paul had stated, in the first chapter, that the false brothers disturb them and want to pervert the gospel of Christ (verse 7) . In a letter sent by the apostles and elders to the churches, it was said: "We have heard that without our permission, some have left and disturbed us, and have clouded their spirits with their words" (Acts 15:24) .

Later, there were still many more of that class. So negative was his work, that the apostle affirmed that everyone who gave himself over to it: "be condemned" (see Galatians 1: 8, 9) . These preachers were deliberately seeking to undermine the gospel of Christ, and thereby destroy believers.

The false brothers were saying, "If you are not circumcised according to the rite of Moses, you cannot be saved" (literally: you have no power to be saved). They degraded salvation to the level of something merely human, something dependent on human power. They did not know what circumcision really consists in: "It is not Jewish what is outwardly, nor is it circumcision which is outwardly, in the flesh, but Jew is he who is inwardly, and circumcision which is of the heart, in the Spirit , not according to the letter, and whose praise does not come from men, but from God. " (Romans 2:28 and 29) .

Tuesday

After relying on God, Abraham once listened to Sarah's voice, instead of listening to the Lord, and tried to fulfill God's promises through the power of his own flesh (Genesis 16) . The result was failure: instead of obtaining an heir, he obtained a slave. God then appeared to him again, urging him to walk before him with a whole heart, and repeated his covenant to him. In order to remember the failure, and the fact that the flesh is of no avail , Abraham received the seal of circumcision, the stripping of a portion of the flesh. This was to show that since the flesh "does not dwell in goodness" , God's promises can be made real when "we strip ourselves of sins" (Colossians 2:11), through the Spirit. "We are the true circumcision, we who worship according to the Spirit of God, and do not trust in the flesh" (Philippians 3: 3) .

Then, when Abraham received the Spirit by faith in God, he was truly circumcised. "And they received circumcision by the way, as a seal of justice for the faith they had when they were uncircumcised" (Romans 4:11) . External circumcision was never anything more than a mere external sign of the authentic circumcision of the heart. What a glorious truth, that where sin abounded, there is Christ, the Savior of the sinner. He takes away sin, all sin, the sin of the world.

Sunday

After accepting the gospel, the Galatians were going astray following false teachers who presented them with "another gospel" , a falsification of the true and unique, as there was never another, at any time, for all men.

The falsification of the gospel was expressed in the following terms: "If you are not circumcised according to the rite of Moses, you cannot be saved" (Acts 15: 1) . Although the subject of the circumcision rite is irrelevant today, nevertheless, with regard to salvation itself, the controversy over whether human works participate or is only for Christ, is as alive as ever.

Instead of attacking error and combating it with powerful arguments, the apostle refers to an experience that illustrates the topic under discussion. In his presentation he demonstrates to them that salvation is only by faith for all men, and in no way by works. Just as Christ tasted death for everyone, everyone who is saved must have the personal experience of Christ's death, resurrection and life in him. Christ in the flesh did what the law was unable to do (Galatians 2:21; Romans 8: 3, 4). But the same fact bears witness to the justice of the law. If you are out in some deficient detail, Christ would not have fulfilled your requirements. Christ shows the righteousness of the law by fulfilling it, or doing what the law requires, not just for us, but in us. God's grace in Christ testifies to the majesty and holiness of the law. We do not discard the grace of God: if justice could be obtained by the law, then Christ died in vain .

To claim that the law can be abolished, that its requirements can be taken into account with little regard, that we can pass them by, is tantamount to saying that Christ died in vain. Let us repeat: justice cannot be obtained by the law, but only by the faith of Christ. But the fact that the justice of the law cannot be achieved in any other way than by the crucifixion, resurrection and life of Christ in us, demonstrates the infinite greatness and sanctity of the law.

O foolish Galatians! Who fascinated you not to obey the truth, to you, before the eyes of whom Jesus Christ was already represented as crucified?

In other words: disobedience, rejecting the Lord, is the spirit of the antichrist. As we have already seen, the Galatian brothers were separating from God. Inevitably, although perhaps without realizing it, they were returning to idolatry.

A protection against spiritism - Spiritism is nothing more than another way of referring to ancient witchcraft, or witchcraft. It is a fraud, but not the kind of fraud that many imagine. There is a reality in it. It is a fraud, since intending to maintain communication with the spirits of the dead, they maintain it only with the spirits of demons, given that "the dead know nothing" . To be a spiritist medium is to surrender to the control of demons.

There is only one way to protect yourself from it, and that is to hold on to the Word of God. Whoever takes the Word of God lightly, is losing his fellowship with God, and is under the influence of Satan. Even the one who denounces spiritism in the most energetic conditions, if he ceases to cling to the Word of God, sooner or later he will be deceived by the powerful seduction of Christ's forgery. Only by standing firm in the Word of God, can the believer be kept from the hour of trial that is to come to all the world (Revelation 3:10) . "The spirit that now works in the children of disobedience" (Ephesians 2: 2) is the spirit of Satan, the spirit of the antichrist; and the gospel of Christ, which reveals the righteousness of God (Romans 1:16 and 17) it is the only possible salvation.

Christ, crucified before us - When Paul preached to the Galatians, he presented Christ crucified. So vivid was the description, that the Galatians were indeed able to contemplate Him before their eyes like the Crucified One. It was not a matter of mere rhetoric on Paul's part, nor of imagination on their part. Using Paul as an instrument, the Holy Spirit qualified them to see Christ crucified.

In this respect, the experience of the Galatians cannot be exclusive to them. The cross of Christ is a current fact. The expression "Going to the cross" , is not a mere form of expression, but something that you can literally fulfill.

No one can know the reality of the gospel until he sees Christ crucified before his eyes, until he sees the cross in each part. It may be that someone finds grace, but the fact that a blind person does not see the sun, and denies that it shines, will not convince what he sees and receives its light. Who are the righteous? - When we read the statement: the just will live by faith , it is essential that we clearly understand what the term "just" means . To be justified by faith is to be made righteous by faith. All injustice is sin (1 John 5:17) , and sin is a transgression of the Law (1 John 3: 4) . Therefore, all injustice is transgression of the law; and of course, all justice is obedience to the law. Saturday

Nobody has bothered me since now; for I bear the marks of the Lord Jesus on my body. The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, brothers, be with your spirit. Amen! (Galatians 6: 17,18)

What has been translated as "signs" is the plural form of the Greek stigma voice. It implies shame and disgrace. In the past, those responsible for crimes, as well as slaves who had been caught trying to escape, were stigmatized by placing a mark or sign on their bodies, indicating to whom they belonged.

Such are the signs of the cross of Christ. Paulo took them. He had been crucified with Christ, and carried the imprints of his nails. They were marked on his body. They marked him as a servant, as the slave of the Lord Jesus. So let no one interfere with him: he was not a servant of men. He owed only loyalty to Christ, who had bought him. Let no one expect to see him serve man or the flesh, because Jesus had marked him with His sign, and could not serve anyone else. No one should interfere in his freedom in Christ or mistreat him either, because his Lord would safely protect those who belonged to Him.

Do you take these marks? Then you can boast about them. If you do so, you will not boast in vain, nor will you be flattered.

How much glory there is on the cross! The entire glory of heaven is in that depreciated object. Not in the figure of the cross, but in the cross itself. The world does not recognize this as glory. But neither did he recognize the Son of God; nor does it acknowledge the Holy Spirit, because they cannot see Christ.

May God open our eyes to see the glory, so that we can recognize its value. May we consent to be crucified with Christ so that the cross will lift us up to glory. On the cross of Christ there is salvation. It is the power of God not to fall, because it lifts us from Earth to heaven. On the cross is the new creation that the same God qualifies as good "in a great way". In it is all the glory of the Father and the entire glory of the eternal ages. Therefore, may God not allow us to glory in anything other than the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, for which the world is crucified to us, and we to the world.
